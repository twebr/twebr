In a world that is changing more rapidly than anything, nothing is more important than delivering a timeless product.

A website is like a rock band: it’s not about making all the elements yourself, it’s about combining them. Do you know a band that plays on self-made guitars? What makes a band unique—and good—is the combination. Combination of instruments, of notes, of musicians. For a website the same thing holds: it’s not about the tools you use, nor about building everything from scratch. It’s about combining existing things.

This is where I come in.

I have a good feeling in typography and the design of user interactions. I also value semantics. I believe designing for the web is not about the looks, but mainly about the meaning and structure of information. I am the combiner: I search for ‘things in existence’ (be it frameworks, scientific research, code snippets or fonts) and put them together to create a great product.

My technical skillset consists of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, PHP, SQL, and object-oriented programming in general. Furthermore, I have experience with graphical software like Photoshop and Illustrator. But perhaps most importantly, I have a large motivation and willingness to learn. I pick up things quickly.

Want to know more about me and my work? Take a look at my portfolio or get in touch.
